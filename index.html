About the STKO Lab

Do you like astronomy, the universe? Great, 'cause we are the astronomers of the information universe. The STKO Lab is an information observatory that investigates the role of Space and Time for Knowledge Organization. Thus, if you like, you can also spell out the STKO acronym as Spatio- Temporal Knowledge Observatory.

Our work spans across different scientific disciplines such as spatial data science, geoinformatics & GIScience, cognitive science, computer science, and the broader earth sciences. While our research interests vary, we typically work on topics related to geospatial semantics, ontologies, knowledge graphs and the Semantic Web, GeoAI and neuro-symbolic AI, (location-based) social networks and volunteered geographic information, geographic information retrieval, Linked Data, Big Data, similarity and analogy-based reasoning, as well as mobile computing and location-based services. Our current big project is the KnowWhereGraph.

A key driver for our work is the question of how to foster the publishing, retrieval, reuse, and integration of data without restricting semantic heterogeneity which we consider a motor of (interdisciplinary) science. We are also very interested in spatially explicit machine learning models (e.g., Space2Vec, Time2Box) for building environmentally intelligent services and coping with ethical dilemmas, and exploratory user interfaces and novel interaction paradigms (e.g., Knowledge Explorer, IOS Press Scientometrics) to browse and navigate unfamiliar data. Methodologically, our niche is the combination of theory-driven (e.g., semantics) and data-driven (e.g., deep learning) approaches.
